# Griot and Grits — Social Media Brand Guide

## Mission
"Preserve the history of the black experience, one voice at a time, through the use of AI and other advanced technologies."

## Primary Tagline
**"Black Voices Worth Remembering, Black History Worth Sharing"**

## Brand Name Origin
- **Griot** — West African hereditary historians who preserve oral traditions through storytelling, poetry, songs, and dance. The griot is the keeper of the community's memory.
- **Grits** — A staple of Southern cuisine, representing Black Southern cultural heritage and the everyday life of the people whose stories are being preserved.

Together: African oral tradition meets Southern Black America.

---

## Voice and Tone

### Core qualities
- **Reverent but alive** — This is not a museum exhibit. These are living people and recent memory.
- **Community-centered** — Speak as a member of the community, not as a narrator from outside it.
- **Specific over general** — Real names, real places, real years carry more weight than abstractions.
- **Earned emotion** — Let the story create the feeling. Don't tell people what to feel.

### Words and phrases that fit the brand
- "our elders," "our people," "our history"
- "the stories they carried," "what they passed down"
- "their words," "their hands," "their sacrifice"
- references to the Great Migration, the church, the land, the school yard, the kitchen table
- "one voice at a time," "before it's too late," "for those who come after us"

### What to avoid
- Corporate-speak: "Check out," "Learn more about," "Discover our platform"
- Passive or abstract framing: "Important stories are being told" → be specific about who and what
- Overclaiming: let the story speak for itself
- Hashtag spam: every tag should earn its place

## Caption Formulas

### Strong hook openers (adapt to the interview content)

**Question-form:**
- "What would you do if you had to leave everything behind?"
- "Do you know where your family was 70 years ago?"
- "What did our grandparents carry that they never talked about?"

**Statement-form:**
- "Some stories don't get told. This is one of them."
- "She lived through history they don't teach in school."
- "He built something from nothing. And he wants you to know how."

**Scene-setting:**
- "Mississippi. 1952. A Black family making a way."
- "Before Google Maps, before cell phones — before a lot of things — there was a woman who knew exactly where she was going."

### Call-to-action options
- "Watch now → griotandgrits.org"
- "Full interview at griotandgrits.org 🔗"
- "Link in bio → hear the full story"
- "Their story deserves to be heard. griotandgrits.org"
